Interview Preparation Center

Technical Interview Checklist

Q1: Explain the difference between SQL and NoSQL databases.

SQL databases are relational, table-based, with fixed schema and structured query language. NoSQL databases are non-relational, document-based, graph-based, or key-value structures with dynamic schemas.

Q2: Explain how a REST API works.

REST (Representational State Transfer) APIs use HTTP methods (GET, POST, PUT, DELETE) to interact with resources identified by URLs, passing payloads in JSON/XML format.

Q3: What is a deadlock and how do you prevent it?

A deadlock is a state where two or more processes are blocked forever, each waiting for resources held by another. Prevent it by eliminating circular wait conditions or resource holding requirements.

HR & Behavioral Checklist

Q1: Tell me about yourself.

Structure your response using the Present-Past-Future framework: describe your current profile/studies, key projects completed in the past, and career alignment goals for this position.

Q2: Why do you want to join our company?

Highlight the engineering culture, specific product challenges, or recent industry announcements showing your interest aligns with the company's growth.

Q3: Describe a challenging project you built.

Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to clearly explain tech bottlenecks and the specific steps you took to resolve them.
